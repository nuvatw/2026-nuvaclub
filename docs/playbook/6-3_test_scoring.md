# Test: Pass or Retry

> **Section ID:** `test-scoring`
> **Parent:** Test
> **Purpose:** Understanding scoring and retake policies

---

## TL;DR

Pass = get above threshold (60-80% depending on level). Fail = try again. Unlimited retries. Only your best score counts.

---

## Passing Thresholds

| Level | Pass Score |
|-------|------------|
| Lv.1-3 | 60% |
| Lv.4-6 | 65% |
| Lv.7-9 | 70% |
| Lv.10-12 | 80% |

---

## How Scoring Works

### Objective Questions (True/False, Multiple Choice)
- Correct = full points
- Incorrect = 0 points
- No partial credit

### Short Answer
- AI evaluates semantic similarity
- Close matches = partial credit
- Key terms matter
- Spelling is somewhat flexible

### Essay
- Rubric-based evaluation
- Multiple criteria scored
- Partial credit possible
- Reasoning valued over length

## Retakes

### Retry Policy
- **Unlimited attempts** - Keep trying until you pass
- **No penalty** - Failed attempts don't hurt you
- **Best score saved** - We only record your highest
- **Same questions?** - No, questions vary each attempt

### Waiting Period
- No waiting period between attempts
- Take it again immediately if you want
- Recommended: Review material first

## Tips for Passing

### Before the Test
1. Complete the matching course
2. Review key concepts
3. Get a good night's sleep
4. Find a quiet place

### During the Test
1. Read questions carefully
2. Manage your time
3. Answer all questions (no penalty for guessing)
4. Review before submitting

### If You Fail
1. Don't stress - you can retry
2. Review your weak areas
3. Re-watch relevant course sections
4. Try again when ready

---

## FAQ

### Can I see which answers I got wrong?
After submitting, you'll see your score and general areas to improve. Specific answers aren't revealed to maintain test integrity.

### What if I run out of time?
Unanswered questions count as wrong. Your score is calculated on what you submitted.

### Is there a cost to retake?
No. Retakes are free and unlimited.

### Do failed attempts show on my profile?
No. Only your best score is recorded and displayed.
